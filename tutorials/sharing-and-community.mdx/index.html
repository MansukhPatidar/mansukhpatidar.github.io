Sharing & Community

NoWires.AI isn’t just a tool — it’s a community of builders. This tutorial shows you how to share your work, discover others’ projects, and build on top of existing designs.

What you’ll learn

  • How to publish a project
  • How thumbnails are generated
  • Using tags for discoverability
  • Browsing the Community drawer
  • Searching and filtering projects
  • Liking, forking, and commenting on projects

1. Publish a project

To make one of your projects visible to everyone:

  1. Open the Project Organizer.
  2. Select the project you want to share.
  3. Toggle the project’s visibility to Public.

That’s it — your project is now live in the community browser for everyone to see.

💡 Tip: Add a clear description and relevant tags before publishing. It helps others understand what your circuit does and find it in search.

2. Automatic thumbnail generation

When you publish a project, NoWires.AI automatically generates a thumbnail image from your board layout. This thumbnail appears in:

  • The community browser
  • Search results
  • Your public profile

No screenshots needed — the thumbnail updates whenever you save changes to a public project.

3. Tags for discoverability

Tags are how people find your project. When publishing, add tags that describe:

  • What it does — “led-driver”, “motor-controller”, “audio-amp”
  • Key components — “555-timer”, “arduino”, “op-amp”
  • Difficulty level — “beginner”, “advanced”
  • Category — “power-supply”, “sensor”, “display”

Multiple tags help your project appear in more searches.

4. Browse the Community drawer

Click the Community icon in the sidebar to open the community browser. You’ll see a feed of public projects from all NoWires.AI users, showing:

  • Thumbnail preview
  • Project name
  • Author
  • Tags
  • Like count

Scroll to browse, or use search to find something specific.

5. Search and filter

At the top of the Community drawer:

  • Search bar — type keywords to find projects by name, description, or tags
  • Filters — narrow results by category or difficulty

Results update as you type, making it fast to find exactly what you need.

6. Like a project

See something you appreciate? Click the heart icon on any community project to like it. Likes help surface great projects and give creators recognition.

7. Fork a project

Forking copies someone else’s public project into your own library:

  1. Open a community project.
  2. Click Fork.
  3. The project appears in your Project Organizer as a personal copy.

Your fork is completely independent — you can modify it freely without affecting the original.

💡 Tip: Forking is the fastest way to learn. Find a circuit similar to what you want to build, fork it, then modify it to your needs.

8. Edit your forked copy

After forking:

  1. Open the project from your Project Organizer.
  2. Edit it like any other project — add components, change wiring, update properties.
  3. Save your changes.

You can even publish your modified version as a new public project.

9. Comments

Community projects support comments:

  • View comments — scroll below a community project to see discussion
  • Add a comment — type your message and post it

Comments are great for asking questions about a circuit, suggesting improvements, or thanking the creator.

What you learned

  • Publishing projects by toggling visibility to Public
  • How thumbnails are auto-generated from your board
  • Using tags to make projects discoverable
  • Browsing, searching, and filtering community projects
  • Liking projects to show appreciation
  • Forking projects to create your own editable copy
  • Adding and reading comments on community projects
