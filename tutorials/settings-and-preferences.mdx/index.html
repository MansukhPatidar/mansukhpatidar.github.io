Settings & Preferences

NoWires.AI has a few settings that let you tailor the experience to how you work. This quick tutorial covers all of them.

What you’ll learn

  • How to open Settings
  • Switching between light and dark mode
  • Toggling pin labels
  • The “Move with connections” option
  • Account and profile settings

1. Open Settings

Click the gear icon at the bottom of the sidebar. The Settings panel opens with all available options.

2. Theme: light vs dark mode

NoWires.AI supports two themes:

  • Dark mode (default) — easier on the eyes for long sessions, high contrast against the green board
  • Light mode — brighter interface, preferred for well-lit environments

Switch between them in the Settings panel. The change applies immediately.

💡 Tip: Dark mode is popular with engineers working in the evening. Light mode can be easier to read in direct sunlight.

3. Pin labels

Toggle Pin Labels to show or hide component pin names on the board:

  • On — each pin displays its label (VCC, GND, OUT, etc.) directly on the board
  • Off — pins are shown as plain dots for a cleaner look

Pin labels are especially helpful when wiring unfamiliar ICs or custom components. You can toggle them on while wiring and off when you want a clean screenshot.

4. Move with connections

Toggle Move with connections to control what happens when you drag a component:

  • On (default) — wires move with the component, keeping connections intact
  • Off — the component moves but wires stay in their original positions, effectively disconnecting

Most users keep this on. Turn it off only when you need to reposition a component without disturbing the surrounding wiring.

5. Account settings

The Settings panel also shows your account information:

  • Email — the email address associated with your account
  • Sign out — click to log out of your account

First-time profile setup

The first time you sign in, you may be prompted to set up your profile:

  • Choose a display name
  • This name appears on any projects you publish to the community
